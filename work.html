Igiza — Trailer

“Igiza” is a tale of twin sisters seeking revenge in Nairobi’s cutthroat fashion industry and money laundering world. Serah Ndanu stars as Linda, a successful fashion designer, and Nicole, an imprisoned former criminal. As Nicole sets her plan in motion, both sisters’ lives change. Adapted for Showmax the 13 episodes are now streaming exclusively on the platform. A must-watch for fans of high-stakes drama and intense character development.

Black and Blue — Trailer

Black & Blue is a Kenyan mockumentary series, now streaming on Showmax, the Kenyan Police enlists a civilian as an Officer Commanding Station (OCS) in a bold attempt to rebrand themselves and better appeal to the public. This new OCS brings a unique civilian perspective to policing, turning things upside down at the experimental pilot station. A documentary team is hired to follow them and document the process, but inadvertently reveals the shenanigans the police get up to behind the scenes as they grapple with adjusting to the new reforms.

Footsteps in the dark

An Intimate Examination of Muslim Kenyan’s Mental Health Struggles. This in-depth investigation delves into the experiences of two Muslim Kenyans as they navigate their mental health journeys, including how their struggles are manifested, perceived, and stigmatized within the Islamic community. Join us as we embark on a poignant journey through the complexities of stigma and the devastating effects of abandonment in the face of mental illness.

Vogue

Vogue’s “Cool Girls’ Guide to Nairobi” is a seven-minute, fast-paced city portrait produced by Yare Picture Studios that swaps tourist snapshots for insider swagger, following three Nairobi tastemakers through thrift-market treasure hunts, rooftop DJ sets, and after-dark art pop-ups; shot on RED cameras with vintage Leica glass and finished in a warm, muted palette, the film blends fashion-editorial polish with street-level authenticity and—within a week of release—pulled more than a million cross-platform views and an “Editors’ Pick” badge on Vogue.com.

Computer Aid

“Computer Aid” is a hopeful mini-doc from Yare Picture Studios that shadows a grassroots program delivering refurbished laptops to under-resourced schools: from dusty storerooms where volunteers wipe and re-image devices, to wide-eyed classrooms where kids tap a keyboard for the first time. Shot in crisp handheld close-ups and sun-drenched wides, the film pairs data-driven infographics with candid teacher testimonies, illustrating how a single recycled computer can unlock coding lessons, online libraries, and new futures—turning e-waste into opportunity, one child at a time.
